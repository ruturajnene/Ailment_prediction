<li>For Joint pain:</li>
<ul>
<li>CRYOTHERAPY</li>
<li>HYDROTHERAPY</li>
<li>If the pain is too great, attempt to immobilize the area with a splint or brace. This 4.should not be done in all cases, but only if the pain of movement is too great to bear.</li>
</ul>
<li>For Osteoarthritis(hands, knees, hips):</li>
<ul>
<li>Control Weight</li>
<li>Exercise</li>
<li>Do warm-up exercises before sports, even less vigorous ones such as golf.</li>
<li>Wear properly fitting shoes that provide shock absorption and stability.</li>
<li>Include omega-3 fatty acids in your diet</li>
</ul>
<li>For Thoracic outlet syndrome:</li>
<ul>
<li>Maintain good posture</li>
<li>Take frequent breaks at work to move and stretch</li>
<li>Maintain a healthy weight</li>
<li>Avoid carrying heavy bags over your shoulder</li>
<li>Avoid activities that worsen symptoms, or find ways to adapt activities so that they don't </li>
<li>cause symptoms</li>
<li>Create a work area that allows you to keep good posture and doesn't make symptoms worse</li>
</ul>
</ol>
